About Me

Hi there! I'm Gift Mthombeni, a Final Year Electrical Engineering student at the Cape Peninsula University of Technology (CPUT), specializing in power systems, solar PV, and battery energy storage systems (BESS). I'm passionate about integrating smart technologies into the energy sector to drive reliability, efficiency, and sustainability.

My journey blends a solid foundation in electrical engineering with the creative application of modern software tools. I’ve built and deployed several full-stack web applications where AI is used as an assistive technology—not the end product. These include a bursary management platform, a smart grid fault detection dashboard, a solar performance app, and an automated invoice generator.

I use technologies like Node.js, React.js, MySQL, Firebase, and Bootstrap—combined with AI models, third-party APIs, and data visualization tools—to deliver clean, functional solutions. These apps are hosted on platforms like Render and Azure, and my development workflow involves end-to-end debugging, GitHub version control, REST API development, and responsive UI design.

My core research interests focus on how solar power, energy storage, and predictive intelligence can be optimized to support rural electrification and decentralized grids. After graduation, I plan to register as a Candidate Professional Engineering Technologist and continue developing intelligent energy-focused applications.

Outside of engineering and development, I enjoy discovering Cape Town’s coastal escapes and local coffee shops—my favorite spots to recharge and reflect on new ideas.
